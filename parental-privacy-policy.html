Privacy Policy — BlockerMax Parental Control

Effective Date: 06-Mar-2026

This Privacy Policy (“Policy”) describes how Nanegate (“Company,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects information when you use the BlockerMax Parental Control mobile application (“App”).

By accessing or using BlockerMax Parental Control, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the terms of this Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the App. This policy is specifically designed to address the privacy concerns of parents and guardians monitoring their children's digital activity.

1. Scope of This Policy

This Policy applies to information collected through the BlockerMax Parental Control App, related mobile services, and in-app purchases. It does not apply to information collected offline, through third-party websites, or other applications not controlled by the Company.

2. Data Collection and Child Privacy (COPPA Compliance)

BlockerMax Parental Control is explicitly designed for parents to manage their children's devices. We are committed to protecting the privacy of children in accordance with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and similar global regulations.

  • Parental Consent: The App must be installed and configured by a parent or legal guardian. By configuring the App on a child's device, you consent to the local processing of data required for the App to function.
  • No PII Collection: We do not ask for, collect, transmit to our servers, or sell any Personally Identifiable Information (PII) of children (such as name, email, address, or location).
  • Local Processing: All filtering, blocking, and monitoring activities happen locally on the device. We do not store your child's browsing history or app usage on our cloud servers.

3. Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of data, depending on your platform and permissions granted:

(a) Accessibility Permission (Android)

BlockerMax Parental Control uses the Android Accessibility Service API solely to enforce app and website blocking based on your parental configuration.

  • The service does not collect, transmit, or store screen content or any personally identifiable information (PII) on our servers.
  • All accessibility actions are processed locally on the child's device to provide core blocking functionality.

(b) Notification Access

The App may request permission to access notifications for the purpose of detecting app usage or identifying blocked app activity.

  • Notification data is processed locally only and is never shared with or transmitted to any server.

(c) Analytics and Crash Reporting

We use Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics (services provided by Google LLC) to collect aggregated, anonymized usage statistics and crash reports to improve the app.

This may include:

  • Device information (model, OS version, country)
  • General app interaction data
  • Error diagnostics and performance data

No personal identifiers or child activity logs are collected or linked to this data.

(d) Subscription and Payment Data

We use RevenueCat, Inc. to manage in-app purchases and subscriptions.

RevenueCat may process limited metadata such as:

  • Product identifiers
  • Purchase status (active, expired, refunded)
  • Anonymous user tokens

This data is handled in accordance with RevenueCat’s own privacy policy.

4. Platform Differences

Due to platform-specific permissions and restrictions:

  • On Android devices, the App may utilize Accessibility and Notification permissions to enforce blocking functionality.
  • On iOS devices, certain blocking and monitoring features may be limited due to system restrictions. The App only collects limited, Apple-permitted analytics and device data.

5. How We Use the Data

The information collected is used strictly for the following purposes:

  • To operate and improve the functionality and performance of the App
  • To diagnose and resolve technical issues
  • To manage subscriptions and validate purchases
  • To understand general user behavior in an aggregated and anonymized form
  • To comply with applicable legal or regulatory requirements

We do not use the collected data for targeted advertising, user profiling, or shadow profiling of minors.

6. Data Storage and Security

  • Accessibility and notification data remain entirely on your device and are never transmitted externally.
  • Analytics and diagnostic logs are securely stored on Firebase servers in accordance with Google’s security standards.
  • Data transmission (where applicable) is protected using TLS/HTTPS encryption.

7. Data Retention

We retain anonymized analytics and diagnostic data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Policy.

You can delete all locally processed blocking rules and configurations at any time by uninstalling the App from the child's device.

8. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to:

  • Request access to or deletion of your data via the parent account
  • Withdraw app permissions at any time via your device’s system settings (which may limit protective functionality)
  • Opt out of analytics tracking
